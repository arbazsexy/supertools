How GPA is calculated

GPA stands for Grade Point Average. It is usually calculated by multiplying each subject grade point by its credits, adding the totals together, and dividing by the total number of credits.

How This GPA Calculator Works

This GPA calculator multiplies each subject grade point by its credit value, adds the grade points together, and then divides that total by the full credit load. That gives you a weighted GPA instead of a simple average, which is the method most schools and colleges follow.

That matters because a high-credit subject affects the final GPA more than a low-credit subject. Seeing the full weighted result helps you judge academic performance more realistically.
