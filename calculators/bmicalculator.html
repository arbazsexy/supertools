What BMI means

Body Mass Index, or BMI, uses height and weight to estimate a general adult weight-status category. It is often used as a quick screening tool, not as a full measure of health or body fat.

BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height (m²)

Adult BMI categories

BMI Range Category
Less than 18.5 Underweight
18.5 - 24.9 Normal weight
25.0 - 29.9 Overweight
30.0 or higher Obese

How This BMI Calculator Works

Body Mass Index, or BMI, uses your weight and height to estimate a general adult weight-status category. The calculator converts your height into meters, squares that value, and divides your weight in kilograms by the result.

BMI = weight (kg) / height (m²)

The result is then matched to the common adult BMI ranges so you can see whether it falls into underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese. The page also estimates a general healthy-weight range for your height to add more context.

BMI is most useful when you want a fast, general screening measure based on height and weight. It can help with personal check-ins, fitness progress reviews, and everyday awareness when you want a rough category without doing the math yourself.

What BMI does not tell you

BMI does not directly measure body fat, muscle mass, fitness level, or overall health. Two people can have the same BMI and still have very different body composition. That is why this tool is best used as general guidance, not as a diagnosis.
