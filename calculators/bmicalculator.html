What is Body Mass Index (BMI)?

Body Mass Index, or BMI, is a simple measurement that uses a person's height and weight to estimate whether their body weight falls within a general health category. It is commonly used as a quick screening tool for adults.

The formula for BMI is based on weight in kilograms divided by height in meters squared. This gives a number that is compared with standard adult BMI ranges.

BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height (m²)

A higher or lower BMI may suggest that a person is underweight, at a normal weight, overweight, or obese. However, BMI does not directly measure body fat and should be used as a general guide rather than a medical diagnosis.

BMI Categories for Adults

BMI Range Category
Less than 18.5 Underweight
18.5 – 24.9 Normal weight
