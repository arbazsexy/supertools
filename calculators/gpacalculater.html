What is GPA?

GPA stands for Grade Point Average. It is a numerical value used by educational institutions to summarize a student's academic performance. Different grades are assigned different grade points, and those points are combined using course credits to produce the final GPA.

GPA = Total Grade Points ÷ Total Credits

For example, if you receive higher grades in subjects with more credits, your GPA will increase more than in subjects with fewer credits. This is why credits are an important part of GPA calculation.
