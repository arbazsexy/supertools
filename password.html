What Makes a Password "Strong"?

A strong password is your first line of defense against hackers and brute-force attacks. To create an uncrackable password, you should ensure it meets the following criteria:

  • Length: It should be at least 12–16 characters long. Every additional character makes a password exponentially harder to crack.
  • Complexity: It must include a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special symbols.
  • Unpredictability: Avoid dictionary words, birthdays, or sequential keyboard strokes (like "qwerty").

Best Practices for Password Security in 2026

Even the strongest password won't protect you if you reuse it. Always generate a unique password for every single account. We highly recommend using a dedicated Password Manager (like Bitwarden, 1Password, or your browser's built-in manager) to safely store the passwords you generate here.
