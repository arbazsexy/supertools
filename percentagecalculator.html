How to Calculate Percentages

Whether you are calculating sales tax, figuring out a tip at a restaurant, or measuring the growth of your business, knowing how percentages work is essential. Our tool automates the process, but here is the math behind it.

Finding X% of Y

To find the percentage of a specific number, you multiply the percentage (divided by 100) by the number.

$$ \text{Result} = \left( \frac{\text{Percentage}}{100} \right) \times \text{Total Value} $$

Calculating Percentage Increase

If a price or value goes up, you can find the percentage increase by subtracting the old value from the new value, dividing the result by the old value, and multiplying by 100.

$$ \text{Increase} = \frac{\text{New Value} - \text{Old Value}}{\text{Old Value}} \times 100 $$

Calculating Percentage Decrease

Similarly, if a value drops (like a store discount), you subtract the new value from the starting value, divide by the starting value, and multiply by 100.

$$ \text{Decrease} = \frac{\text{Starting Value} - \text{Ending Value}}{\text{Starting Value}} \times 100 $$
Example: If a shirt originally costs $50 and is now on sale for $40, the decrease is $10. Dividing $10 by the original $50 gives 0.20. Multiply by 100, and you get a 20% decrease.
